## Idea 3 — Why “how much past text is in play” keeps growing

Three everyday situations all lengthen the past the next step must consider:

| What you do                         | What grows for the next token                                        |
| ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| You send a **longer prompt**        | More tokens exist before the first output token                      |
| The model writes a **longer reply** | Each new token joins the past for the step after it                  |
| You continue a **multi-turn chat**  | Earlier turns usually stay in context (unless the product truncates) |

Nothing mystical is happening. The loop always asks “what next, given so far?”—and “so far” is simply longer.

That is why inference systems care so much about **sequence length**. Even before we talk about GPU memory, you can see the product-level fact: longer conversations mean more past text is “in play” for every new word.

## Idea 4 — Two different meanings of “memory”

People use “memory” for two different things. Keep them separate from day one:

1. **Product language** — “The model remembers our conversation.” That usually means: the **text** of earlier turns is still in the **context** the product sends to the model.
2. **Engine language** — Structures a serving system stores on the GPU so it does not **recompute** work for every past token (this course’s later focus: the **KV cache**, starting in lesson 3).

This lesson only needs (1): the **visible, growing text timeline**.

Lesson 2 asks _how_ the model **looks back** along that timeline when choosing the next token.  
Lesson 3 asks _what_ the engine **stores** so looking back is affordable.

## What to take with you

If you remember only three sentences from lesson 1:

1. Generation is a **loop**: next token → append → repeat.
2. Each step conditions on a **longer past** than the step before.
3. “Context” here means **text in play**—not yet GPU caches or attention math.

When those feel obvious, you are ready for lesson 2.
