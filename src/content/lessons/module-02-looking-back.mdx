## Learning goal

Explain attention as **weighted looking-back** over past tokens, and assign plain-language roles to **query, key, and value**.

## Builds on lesson 1

Generation is a loop. When the model is about to write token _t_, earlier tokens may matter a lot, a little, or almost not at all.

## The question attention answers

> For this new position, **how much should I look at** each past position—and **what information** should I take from it?

## Three roles (intuition first)

| Role      | Plain question                                    | Letter |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ------ |
| **Query** | “What am I looking for right now?”                | Q      |
| **Key**   | “How am I findable / what do I match on?”         | K      |
| **Value** | “If you select me, what content do I contribute?” | V      |

Story form: **look up with K, then read out V.**

You do **not** need the full matrix formula in this lesson. Lesson 3 only needs: past positions produce **K and V that might be reused**.

## What the 3D surface shows

The height field is a **demo** attention map (deterministic teaching data, not a real model forward pass):

- **Taller cells** ≈ more attention weight.
- **● markers** ≈ early **attention sinks** (positions that often attract mass—important later for eviction, lesson 8).
- **■ markers** ≈ **heavy hitters** (high cumulative importance in the demo).

Rows are queries; columns are keys. We downsample large grids so the scene stays readable (`docs/primitives.md`).

## Layers and heads (light touch)

- **Layers:** the model stacks many blocks; looking-back happens repeatedly. That multiplies stored K/V in lesson 3.
- **Heads:** several attentions can run in parallel (“perspectives”). Later, **sharing K/V across heads** (GQA/MQA) shrinks cache size—lesson 3.

## Causal masking (one sentence)

In left-to-right generation, a token usually must not attend to **future** positions. Demo matrices use a causal pattern when rows = cols.
