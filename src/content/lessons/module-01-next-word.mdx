## Where we start

You already know the product experience: you type a message, a language model writes a reply. That is enough for this lesson.

What most people have **not** been shown is the shape of the work underneath. The model does not “understand the whole question and then emit a finished paragraph” as one act. It writes the reply **one small piece at a time**, and each piece immediately becomes part of the story for the next piece.

That single idea—_next piece, again and again_—is the foundation of everything later in this course. Memory pressure, KV caches, paging, and quantization only make sense once you can feel how a reply is a **growing timeline**, not a static document.

You do **not** need transformer math, GPUs, or serving jargon yet. Those arrive after this loop is clear.

## Idea 1 — A reply is a sequence of decisions

Imagine the model has already seen your prompt:

> User: “Name three colors of the sky at sunset.”

Before any answer text exists, the model’s job is not “produce three colors.” Its job is narrower and more mechanical:

> Given everything so far, what is a good **next token**?

A **token** is a chunk of text the model treats as one unit—sometimes a whole word (`sky`), sometimes a fragment (`un`, `believ`, `able`), sometimes punctuation. For this course you can think of tokens as **beads on a string**: discrete pieces the model can add one by one.

So the first decision might yield `The`, then `sky`, then `can`, and so on—not a pre-written essay.

## Idea 2 — Each decision becomes part of the past

After the model chooses a next token, that token is **appended** to the text so far. The next decision does not start from a blank slate. It starts from:

**prompt + everything already written in the reply.**

That is the **generation loop**:

1. Look at the text so far (prompt, then growing reply).
2. Choose one next token.
3. Append it.
4. Repeat until the model stops (end of reply, length limit, or a stop signal).

Engineers call this **autoregressive generation**: each step is conditioned on a **growing past**. The name is optional; the loop is not.
